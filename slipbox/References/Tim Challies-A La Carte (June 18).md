## Recommended Reading (Six Picks)

### 1. Don't Let Envy Poison Your Soul

Daniel Darling argues: few sins poison the soul quite as thoroughly as envy.

> "In today's modern culture, we have been conditioned to nurture envy at anyone who is perceived to be ahead of us on the career, economic, or social ladder. **Envy is at the heart of our politics on all sides. Envy fuels the marketing machines.** Just watch the ads. Someone owns a product/gadget/service that is making them happy, and you don't have it. That's not right, we are told, I must have it."

### 2. Biblical Self-Examination: Questions for Growth

Stacey Pardoe on the necessity of biblical self-examination — and the warning that it can be done poorly.

> "Self-examination is a biblical concept, but we can be swept too far into this introspection. We need to always examine ourselves **in the light of God's truth, keeping the Gospel at the center** of our examination."

She offers a series of useful questions to guide Christians in the practice.

### 3. 10 Tips for Becoming an Excellent Bible Interpreter

> "Becoming a skilled interpreter of Scripture is not a complicated task. It is hard, but it isn't complicated. **God does not hide the riches of his Word from the simple; he hides them from the proud and ungodly.** Right interpretation, then, is first a matter of personal character and piety, and then a matter of methodology. Here are ten basic tips."

### 4. Why NOT to Build a Bigger Sanctuary

A church-planter's counter-argument to "our church is full, we need more space":

> "The size and use of your sanctuary will greatly affect the culture of your church moving forward. Let me humbly offer a little different perspective on this topic, especially for those of you in churches that are outgrowing your space. **Here are 5 reasons to potentially NOT build a bigger sanctuary.**"

### 5. Five Points in My Pain — Joni Eareckson Tada

How the doctrines of God's sovereign saving grace sustain her through her trials.

> "Here I am, afflicted with sores and scars, increasing pain, quadriplegia, and the constant threat of deadly pneumonia. How is it, then, that I am consoled by the doctrine of God's absolute dominion over every moment of my pain and paralysis? Why would I even encourage Christians to view God's providence as the ultimate source of great comfort? **The answers are found in the doctrines of grace, those treasured canons of our faith that extol God's sovereignty in our salvation past, present, and future.**"

### 6. Confessing Sin, Then and Now — Steven Wedgeworth

Steven Wedgeworth (relatively new to Anglicanism) goes through different editions of the **Book of Common Prayer** to see how some of its most beautiful prayers and confessions have been softened through the years. (The BCP historically contains excellent examples of both prayers and confessions.)
