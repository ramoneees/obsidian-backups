# Summer Reset Framework: How To Be Productive in June-Aug With 3 Tips

Asian Efficiency Team — The Productivity Show episode 618W.

## Summary

The episode reframes summer productivity slumps as a *systems mismatch*, not a character flaw. The brain literally works differently in June than in January, and pretending otherwise sets you up for friction. The Summer Reset framework is built around three levers:

- **Simplify goals** — fewer, looser commitments over the summer quarter. The density of goals that worked in Q1 will feel oppressive in Q3; pare back to what truly matters.
- **Anchor routines** — keep the non-negotiable anchors (sleep, movement, one keystone habit) even when everything else loosens. Routines are the load-bearing wall; goals are the paint.
- **Move lower-brain tasks to the hottest parts of the day** — leverage circadian rhythm. Reserve peak cognitive slots for deep work, and let admin / chores / inbox sit in the thermal troughs.

## Key Ideas

- **Brain in summer ≠ brain in winter.** Light exposure, social rhythm, and ambient temperature shift dopamine and attention baselines. Work *with* the season, not against it.
- **Goals vs. routines:** goals are seasonal; routines are infrastructure. Don't redesign your whole life in July.
- **Temperature as a productivity variable.** Heat degrades executive function; cool environments (or moving indoors) recover it.
- **Lower-brain tasks = anything routine, reactive, low-creativity** (email, scheduling, expense reports). Move them out of prime hours.
