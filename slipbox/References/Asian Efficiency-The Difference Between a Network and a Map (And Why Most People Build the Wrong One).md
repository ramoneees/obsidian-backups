# The Difference Between a Network and a Map (And Why Most People Build the Wrong One)

After ~10 years of hosting events in Austin (dinners, AI meetups, investor dinners), Thanh has a big network — but the *size* isn't what makes it useful.

> They built a network. They didn't build a map.

## What a Map Is

Knowing what each person in your network is actually looking for: industry focus, stage, check size, deployment status. What introductions they actually want. What problem they're trying to solve this year.

Most people can answer this for ~5 people. They have the contact, not the thesis.

## Why It Matters: Speed

When a new opportunity lands, Thanh can think of 5–10 specific people to text within minutes. Targeted texts, not mass emails. "Hey, this matches exactly what you told me you were looking for." The recipient feels *remembered*, not solicited.

## How It Accumulates

- Pay attention when people say what they want.
- Write it down (mental note for the active circle; formal note for close collaborators).
- Treat what people tell you as **signal, not small talk**.

The homework feels like work — but it's exactly what creates the speed asymmetry when a deal lands.
