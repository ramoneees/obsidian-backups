# Why Your Priorities Keep Getting Skipped (and the Fix That Actually Works)

Coaching story: an entrepreneur client living in a van had three non-negotiables (exercise, team call, outdoor adventure) that kept getting skipped because his week had no shape — "real stuff" always expanded to fill the time.

## Key Idea: Big Rocks First

The classic jar analogy (rocks → pebbles → sand). Most people know it and still don't do it. Priorities end up at the bottom of a list that's never finished.

The fix: identify **three non-negotiables** for the week and **lock them in the calendar before anything else**. The client's week got a "shape" — and constraints felt freeing, not limiting.

## White Space Matters

After locking the rocks, **resist the urge to fill the remaining hours**. An over-scheduled calendar becomes rigid and you miss opportunities you couldn't have predicted. Buffer blocks, empty mornings, unscheduled afternoons = capacity, not wasted time.

## The Ideal Week Framework

At AE, the Ideal Week starts by scheduling the important work first: deep work, exercise, family, recovery. Then meetings, then everything else. Calendar shapes around priorities — not the other way around.
