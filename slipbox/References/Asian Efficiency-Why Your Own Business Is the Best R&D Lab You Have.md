# Why Your Own Business Is the Best R&D Lab You Have

*Case study framing for AI-era consultants — by the Asian Efficiency team.*

## Main Argument

Most consultants and service providers build case studies the slow way: do client work, document results, clean it up, package it in a deck. By the time the case study is ready, it feels dated. There's a faster loop: **solve the problem in your own business first, then the proof and the tool exist at the same time.**

## The Demo-vs-Case-Study Distinction

- **A case study** says "I helped someone else do this." By construction it lags reality.
- **A live tool** says "I use this myself and here is what it does right now." This lands differently in sales conversations.
- Example from the article: a carousel generator built for AE in a weekend using **Lindy** — now one of the consultant's best demos because they run it on their own podcast transcripts weekly.

## The Two-Way Feedback Loop

The author observes this across AI-implementation consultants doing well:

- Tools built for internal use become demos for clients.
- Problems clients bring in suggest what to build next for the internal system.
- Real estate firm engagement → ideas for post-meeting workflows.
- CPA engagement → ideas for email filtering.

**The principle**: client work is also R&D; you're just not logging it that way. One consultant built a talent pool from workshop attendees — his teaching lab became his workforce.

## Practical Test

Before any new consulting/coaching project, ask: **"Would this same solution be useful in my own business?"** Not always, but often. If yes:

1. Build it for yourself first.
2. Document as you go.
3. When it works, you've already done the proof-of-concept work.
4. Bring it to clients as something you use, not just something you've theorized about.

**Your business stops being overhead. It becomes the product.**
