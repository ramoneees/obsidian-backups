# Why Your Good Habits Keep Dying (It's Not a Willpower Problem)

## Summary

When a habit dies, the reflex is to blame discipline. The real cause is usually a **task-energy mismatch**: the workflow demands more energy than the moment you're doing it offers. The fix isn't more willpower — it's reducing friction so the habit works at your worst-case energy level.

## Key Ideas

- **The case study:** Client Ilias built a 5-step YouTube research workflow (NotebookLM → ChatGPT with custom prompt → investment breakdown). Worked great for two weeks; then monotonous, then dead. The breaking point was evening videos after 8 PM when he didn't want to open the computer.
- **Task-energy mismatch.** The task is fine. The energy level required doesn't match the energy available when you actually want to do it. People design habits for their best-case self; habits have to work for their worst-case self too.
- **Every step is a decision point.** At low energy, every decision point is an exit ramp. Five steps can feel like fifty.
- **The Friction Audit:** count the steps in the workflow. Ask "which of these steps could disappear?" — not faster, just *not exist*.
- **Automation Spectrum (AE framework):** life automation (routines, recurring decisions) → digital automation (templates, shortcuts, filters) → AI automation (review, synthesis, drafting). Goal: automate the backstage so the front stage (thinking, insight, decision) happens without friction.
- **The fix for Ilias:** Lindy picks up new YouTube videos, transcribes, runs the custom prompt, posts to Slack. He just checks Slack. Same information, different experience.
- **Sustainable habits ask little of you.** Designed to work when tired, distracted, or unmotivated. The lower the energy required, the more consistent the habit.

## Aplicação Prática

- Audite cada hábito/workflow que você abandonou. Conte os passos. Quantos podem sumir?
- Para workflows digitais noturnos: empurre a saída para onde você já está (Slack, email, mobile).
- Pergunta de teste: "isso funciona às 22h quando estou exausto?" Se a resposta for não, simplifique.
