# The Doctor Who's Already Using AI (And What She Uses It For)

## Summary

Dr. Vo (clinician) was already using AI daily — not for diagnosis or labs, but for the **20-minute counseling narrative** she had to write after every patient visit. She provides the clinical points; Gemini/ChatGPT/OpenEvidence produces 5 patient-friendly sentences. Same quality, 20 minutes back per patient. This illustrates how AI adoption actually happens: bottom-up, one boring task at a time, not top-down transformation.

## Key Ideas

- **The task:** After every patient visit, Dr. Vo wrote a counseling narrative — lifestyle recommendations, weight guidance, dietary changes. Clear, personalized, clinically accurate. Important, but repetitive in structure. ~20 min per visit.
- **The fix:** AI generates the 5-sentence summary from her clinical bullet points. She reviews and adjusts. **She kept all clinical judgment to herself.**
- **The pattern across professions:** Doctors write. Lawyers write. Consultants write. Engineers write reports. Accountants write memos. The expertise is in the *judgment*; the writing is just the container around the thinking.
- **AI eliminates overhead, not expertise.** Clinical diagnosis, lab interpretation, judgment calls — untouched. Only the translation layer (technical → accessible prose) is automated.
- **How it spreads:** No top-down mandate. No consultant transformation project. One boring task someone tried on a Tuesday afternoon. The principle: **Start Small, Iterate**.
- **Find your 20-minute task.** Most professional jobs have repetitive tasks that require expertise to set up but are formulaic in execution: summaries, translations of technical findings, draft emails. These are AI's sweet spot.

## Aplicação Prática

- Identifique tarefas profissionais que exigem expertise para "setup" mas são repetitivas na execução. Doc, analise, automatize.
- O critério: AI segura a estrutura, você segura o julgamento. Nunca delegue o juízo clínico/estratégico.
- Adoção orgânica > transformação top-down. Comece com UMA tarefa. Funciona → repete.
