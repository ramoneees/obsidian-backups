## Resumo

Digest diário do Tim Challies curando artigos e links de interesse. Edição de hoje cobre:

**Sales & Deals (Kindle):** commentary on Ephesians, Powlison's _Seeing with New Eyes_, Ortlund's _What It Means to Be Protestant_, outros.

**Recommended Reading:**

1. **How We Tell the Story of Down Syndrome** — Amy Julia Becker em The Dispatch: "The way we tell the story of Down syndrome—the language we use, the statistics we present, and the anecdotes we share—shapes the choices we make, individually and collectively, to reject or receive the lives of people with Down syndrome among us." A linguagem que usamos sobre pessoas com Down syndrome importa porque molda decisões sociais e culturais. (Gift Link)

2. **The Comfort & Assurance of Baby Steps in Sanctification** — Gentle Reformation: "When we think of baby steps, we don't think of Usain Bolt. We think of cute little kids waddling forward. They're not fast. They're not even steady, but they're making progress. There's at least one thing that a toddler and Usain Bolt have in common when 'running'—they're both moving forward." Encouragement pra quem está no meio do slow process de santificação — a lentidão não é fracasso, é o processo normal.

3. **Young Man: What Are You Good At?** — Zak Mellgren: artigo pra jovens (e não-tão-jovens) homens sobre ter **functional knowledge or ability in the real world that serves or blesses others in real and tangible ways**. Distingue de habilidades em worlds virtuais (Fortnite, Minecraft) — o ponto é servir o real.

4. **We Don't Hate and Then Harm—We Harm and Then Hate** — Trevin Wax (TGC): "We live in a world of ceaseless conflict. And when we look for the source of that conflict, we often assume it's hatred. We hate people and then treat them poorly. It's because we feel contempt toward others that we sin against them. But that's only half the story. Often it's the other way around. First, we hurt someone, and only then do we begin to hate them." A causalidade moral é mais complicada do que "ódio causa dano". Às vezes dano gera ódio. Importante pra entender conflito interpessoal.

5. **Discipleship Does Not Equal Shepherding: A Crucial Distinction in the Female Pastor Debate** — Jonathon Woodyard (Christ Over All): diferencia "the call of every Christian to make disciples and the call of some to serve the church in the office of pastor". Distinção crucial no debate sobre pastoras femininas — todo cristão é chamado a fazer discípulos, mas nem todo cristão é chamado ao office específico de pastor.

6. **Boy Trip 2026** — David Hall (Reformed Politics): descrição do seu "Boy Trip" recente com os netos. "Seize the most of every opportunity, parents and grands, because the children will grow up and we will grow older. So we do what's important while we can. Don't put off using your summer for family discipleship. Then build on that after summer." Urgência de usar summer pra family discipleship enquanto é possível.
