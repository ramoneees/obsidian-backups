# Don't Fight the Friction — Remove It Before It Starts

Coaching client (entrepreneur in a van) couldn't get to the gym consistently — 9am call + drive left no margin. His solution: **sleep in the gym parking lot the night before**.

He wakes up already there. No commute, no negotiation with himself. My response: "That's the key insight right there."

## Willpower Is the Wrong Variable

Productivity advice implicitly assumes you'll make a good decision when tired, stressed, or behind. That's the wrong target. The real variable is **friction** — the gap between where you are and where the behavior needs to happen.

> Friction is the raw material for the excuse. Remove the friction, and you remove the excuse.

## What It Looks Like

Laying out gym clothes the night before. Shoes by the door. Coffee prepped. Alarm in another room. These work because they **eliminate the decision** before you'd have to make it. "Setting an elaborate trap for yourself."

## Cognitive Work Too

If you've been procrastinating on a project, ask: "what's the friction actually stopping me?" Missing info, annoying platform, vague first step. Solve the structural problem — motivation will follow.
