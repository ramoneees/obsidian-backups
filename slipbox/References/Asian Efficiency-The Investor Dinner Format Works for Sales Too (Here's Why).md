# The Investor Dinner Format Works for Sales Too (Here's Why)

## Summary

The investor dinner format — hosting 30 investors in a casual group setting rather than scheduling 30 individual meetings — applies equally to B2B sales and biz dev. The core dynamic is **group social acceleration**: when one buyer signals interest, the others notice, and FOMO replaces stalling.

## Key Ideas

- **Group dynamics beat isolation.** Most sales processes are designed for one-on-one meetings, where the prospect can stall indefinitely. A curated group format creates productive tension: nobody wants to be the one who passed on the deal everyone else was excited about.
- **The access problem solves at scale.** Getting a one-on-one with a CMO is hard. A curated dinner with their peers — framed as a knowledge-sharing experience rather than a sales pitch — is an easier ask.
- **Social proof runs in real time.** The room's reaction is visible. In a Zoom call, you can't manufacture that.
- **Follow-up starts warmer.** Individual post-dinner conversations don't start from zero. The ask feels like a natural next step.
- **The format itself signals quality.** Anyone can schedule a Zoom. Not everyone hosts a curated dinner.
- **The core insight:** most high-stakes sales and biz dev is still done through one-on-one meetings when a group format would be faster and more effective.

## Aplicação Prática

- Se você vende para CMOs / CTOs / executivos, considere hospedar jantares curatoriais de 20-30 prospects ideais em vez de reuniões individuais.
- Posicione como "peer knowledge-sharing", não como pitch de vendas.
- O follow-up individual depois fica muito mais curto — a dinâmica de grupo fez o trabalho pesado.
