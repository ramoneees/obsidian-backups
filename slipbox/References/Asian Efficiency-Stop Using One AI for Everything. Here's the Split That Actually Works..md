# Stop Using One AI for Everything. Here's the Split That Actually Works

Every AI tool does one of two jobs. Mixing them up is the most common mistake.

## The Two Jobs

1. **Thinking** — the problem isn't fully formed. Brainstorm, explore, push back, ask questions. ChatGPT / Claude in chat mode. Reasoning quality matters.
2. **Executing** — you know what you want and need it to happen. Email, docs, tracker updates, tool actions. Lindy, n8n, purpose-built agents.

## The Two-Step Workflow

1. Work out the idea in chat (ChatGPT or Claude).
2. Pass the final spec to your agent tool: "do this, create this, send this."

Concrete example: meeting transcript → ChatGPT for summary + decisions → super agent for "create Google Doc, email team, update tracker, create Todoist tasks." 12 seconds vs 30 minutes.

## Test for Your Stack

For every AI tool: is this where I **think**, or where I **execute**?

A film producer coached by AE ran each idea through three models in sequence (ChatGPT → Claude → Gemini) — not indecision, strategy. Each gave something the others missed.
