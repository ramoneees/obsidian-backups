# Play Before the Throne

Clinton Manley reflects on why adults struggle to play — his own toddlers far surpass him in the "happy talent" of knowing how to play, part of the "arduous discipline of hedonism" (C.S. Lewis, *Letters to Malcolm*).

## What Is Play?

Aquinas: play is "words or deeds wherein nothing further is sought than the soul's delight" (*Summa Theologica* 2.168.2). Play is **rest for the soul** — recreation that makes us new.

The Hebrew *śāḥaq* (most often translated "play") comes from "to laugh." **Play is to action as laughter is to sound.**

## Playfulness as a Virtue

The ancients called it *eutrapelia* — the habit of using play for recreation, but with wisdom and temperance. The mean between two vices:
- *Playlessness* (Scrooge)
- *Play gluttony* (the gamer who never leaves the basement)

Kevin Gushiken (*A Theology of Play*): "Play is the God-given ability and permission to fully enjoy moments in life as God intended, with freedom and pleasure."

## A Playful God

Scripture reveals a triune God who is the source and sovereign of merrymaking. The world began in wise play (Prov 8:30–31), is full of laughter and play (Job 39; Ps 104:26), and will end in childlike play (Zech 8:5; Isa 11:8; Mal 4:2).

As image-bearers, we imitate his playfulness.
