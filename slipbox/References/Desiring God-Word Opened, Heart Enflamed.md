# Word Opened, Heart Enflamed

John Piper — Light + Truth podcast, classic sermon. **Episode 837**, Part 2 of the series *When the Word Ignites Joy*.

## Core Text

**2 Timothy 4:2** — "Preach the word; be ready in season and out of season; reprove, rebuke, and exhort, with complete patience and teaching."

## Central Question

What kind of preaching makes Christ look *supremely satisfying*?

## Argument

Urgent preaching awakens hearts to treasure Christ above all. The "preach the word" charge is not neutral information delivery — it is the public demonstration that Christ is more satisfying than whatever the listener is currently chasing. The urgency (in season, out of season) is itself part of the message: if Christ really is supreme, then delay is itself a kind of theft from the hearer.

## Preaching Pattern

- **Reprove** — name what contradicts the truth
- **Rebuke** — sharper correction where needed
- **Exhort** — call to action / pursuit
- **With complete patience and teaching** — the manner, not just the content

## Series Context

Part 1 (Episode 836): "God Commands Our Joy" — the theological foundation that joy in God is both commanded and commanded because it is the highest good.

Part 2 (this episode): the *mechanism* by which that joy gets ignited — through opened Scripture applied with urgency.
