# How to Save a Marriage

*Ask Pastor John #2264 — John Piper on Philippians 2:4 as the marriage-saving prayer.*

## The Setup

Most people enter relationships with an invisible ledger. What we do for others, we track. What we are owed, we track. When debts are unpaid, we grow distant.

Piper claims there is a phrase in the Bible with the sheer power to heal broken friendships and save countless hurting marriages. The catch: it requires doing something that goes against every natural, self-protective instinct.

## The Tweet That Started This Episode (2018)

> "How many marriages could be saved, broken friendships reconciled, families held together, if we all looked not only to our own interests but also to the interests of others?" — John Piper

The verse behind the tweet: **Philippians 2:3–4** — "Do nothing from selfish ambition or conceit, but in humility count others more significant than yourselves. Let each of you look not only to his own interests, but also to the interests of others."

## The Key Phrase

> "**The interests of others**" — those four words can save a marriage, heal a broken friendship, hold together a family.

Verse 4 is perhaps the most recurring prayer Piper prays for himself. He first admitted this in a 2008 sermon called "The Mind of Christ" — extraordinary sermon on Philippians 2:4–5.

## The Practical Heart

Piper's most recurrent prayer for himself:

> "That God would do such a deep work in me that I would not only look 'to my own interests, but also to the interests of others.' I just want to be less selfish. I want to be more other-oriented. I want that to be a more default posture in me. I don't want to have to work at it as hard. I would like it to grow up in me — because I think if we as a church could become verse 4, I just think we'd be so wise. **God loves to bestow wisdom about how to live when verse 4 is happening in a church.**"

## Word Note

The word "interests" in "your own interests" / "the interests of others" is **a filler** — not in the original Greek. The Greek is just "your own" and "the others." The paraphrase: *"Let each of you look not only to his own [things], but also to the [things] of others."*

## Application (Implicit)

- Stop tracking the ledger.
- Make "look to the interests of others" a default posture, not a heroic effort.
- The prayer is for the deep work that makes other-orientation a reflex.
- Wisdom about daily life flows naturally where verse 4 is happening in community.
